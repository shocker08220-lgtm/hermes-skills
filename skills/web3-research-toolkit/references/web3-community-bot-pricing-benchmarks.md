# Web3 / Telegram Community Bot Pricing Benchmarks

Use this when Ian asks how to price Sentinel or compare Telegram/Discord/Web3 community bots. Treat numbers as public-surface benchmarks, not audited company metrics. Most tools do **not** disclose paid user counts; infer only with explicit assumptions.

## Research workflow

1. Search official pricing / FAQ pages first.
2. Search Telegram bot landing pages (`t.me/<bot>`) for monthly users when relevant.
3. Search third-party traffic/company pages only as weak signals.
4. Separate **usage** from **monetization**:
   - MAU / active groups / protected members = usage.
   - paid users / paid groups / MRR / ARR = monetization.
5. If paid users are not disclosed, say “未公开” and provide a range only if there is a defensible proxy: public MRR, ARR, active groups × conversion assumption, or plan price.
6. For Telegram bot DAU estimates, use DAU/MAU 10–30% as a rough range and label it as estimate.

## Public benchmark snapshot from the Sentinel pricing research session

### Combot
- Official usage: ~190k active groups, ~16M active users, ~40M messages/day.
- Pricing: free small tier; Pro often cited around ~$5/group/month.
- Paid users: not public. Conservative modeling: 1–5% of active groups paid → ~1.9k–9.5k paid groups; at $5/group/mo → ~$9.5k–47.5k MRR.
- Positioning lesson: mature low-ARPU utility; strong analytics/rules/CAS, but traditional and per-group.

### Miss Rose / Rose clones
- Official FAQ usage: ~1B total users, ~24M total groups, ~55M monthly active users, ~2M monthly active groups.
- Telegram page signal: @missrose_bot around ~2.18M monthly users.
- Pricing: core Rose free; custom clone product via Believable Bots: Core ~$10/mo, Premium ~$50/mo.
- Paid users: not public. Monetization is branded/private bot instances, not core moderation.
- Positioning lesson: enormous free infra; paid hook is white-label/custom instance.

### GroupHelp
- Telegram page signal: @GroupHelpBot around ~1M monthly users; some clones can show hundreds of thousands monthly users.
- Pricing: free core; guide mentions PRO for 5 groups via minimum donation ~€1.50; third-party estimates often cite ~$3–4/group/mo.
- Paid users: not public; ARPU likely low/donation-like.
- Positioning lesson: huge utility usage but weak SaaS monetization signal.

### Varta
- Official usage signal: ~48 communities, ~29k protected members.
- Pricing: $5 Hobby, $19 Starter, $49 Pro, $99 Business; annual ~20% off.
- Paid users: not public; early-stage. If 20–60% of 48 communities paid → ~10–30 paid communities.
- Positioning lesson: smaller usage but clearer AI anti-spam SaaS willingness-to-pay.

### Watchdog.chat
- Pricing: roughly $24–59 Launch, $49–99 Scale/Pro, enterprise ~$416+/mo yearly equivalent.
- Public MRR signal from a 2025 no-code newsletter: ~$100 MRR at that time.
- Paid users: from $100 MRR and $24–99 plans → roughly 1–4 customers at that snapshot.
- Positioning lesson: AI moderation is real but hard to cold-start; sell a specific pain, not generic bot features.

### Collab.Land
- Public usage: 50k+ tokenized communities, ~3.6M unique wallets; historical claims include ~44k daily “Let’s Go” clicks and thousands of new wallets/day.
- Pricing: Free, $17.99, $35, $149, $449, Enterprise.
- Paid users: not public; many communities remain free. Model carefully by paid-community conversion assumptions only.
- Positioning lesson: Web3 infra trust layer; pricing anchored to verified members, not total chat members.

### Guild.xyz
- Public usage: trusted by 1000+ teams; major case studies with hundreds of thousands of members.
- Pricing: $29 Starter, $99 Plus, $399 Growth.
- Paid users: not public. If 10–30% of 1000+ teams paid → ~100–300 paid teams (speculative).
- Positioning lesson: growth/gating/identity platform, not moderation bot; higher pricing is justified by business outcome.

### Collony / Phantom Mod / AI Web3 CM tools
- Collony pricing: about $49 / $99 / $199 with message/day limits and TG/DC AI moderation/chat coverage.
- Phantom Mod: around $200/mo for whitepaper-trained AI community manager; more service/DFY than pure SaaS.
- Paid users: generally not public.
- Positioning lesson: Web3 AI CM tools anchor against human CM cost ($300–$5k/mo), not bot utilities.

## Sentinel pricing implication

Do not position Sentinel as a cheaper Combot/Rose. Position it as a lightweight community operating system: TG + Discord + AI + growth + tickets + tasks + points + shop + analytics.

Recommended early pricing frame:
- Free: $0, one community/basic limits, used to remove install friction.
- Pro: $14.9/mo, active community plan.
- Annual: $143/year (20% off from $14.9 × 12).
- Buyout: $299 one-time for current Pro features; exclude unlimited future AI-heavy costs.

Best pricing anchor line:
> Hiring a part-time community manager costs $300–1000/mo. Sentinel starts at $14.9/mo.

Early growth metric should be paid communities, not DAU:
- 10 paid communities: prove willingness to pay.
- 50 paid communities: validate product direction.
- 100 paid communities: SaaS traction begins.
- 300 paid communities: consider Team/Agency/Enterprise tiers.
